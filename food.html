Food for Dementia

  1. Fruits and vegetables: Colorful fruits and vegetables are a good source of vitamins and minerals. Foods with carotenoids
    • carrots
    • squash
    • leafy greens(spinach,
    • sweet potato
    • cantaloupe (melon)
    • tomatoes
    • bell pepper
    • watermelon
    • apricot
  2. Fish: Oily fish with a good source of protein, vitamins, and minerals, including B3, which may help the brain and nervous system function well. (omega-3 fatty acids)
    • salmon
    • mackerel
    • tuna
    • sardines
    • herring
  3. Beans, nuts, and seeds: These are a good source of vitamin B6, which may help regulate sleep and is important for brain health.
  4. Berries: Berries are a good source of antioxidants and flavonoids, which may help prevent cell damage and slow brain damage.
    • Blueberries: Research from the University of Cincinnati suggests that blueberries may lower the risk of developing late-life dementia
    • Strawberries: Contain pelargonidin, an anthocyanidin that may reduce oxidative stress and improve memory
    • Blackberries: Contain antioxidants and flavonoids that may help prevent cell damage and slow brain damage
  5. Whole grains: Whole grains are an important part of a healthy diet.
    • brown rice
    • wholemeal bread
    • oats
    • quinoa
    • barley
    • farro
  6. Low-fat dairy products: Low-fat dairy products are an important part of a healthy diet.
    • skim milk
    • low-fat yogurt
    • low-fat cheese
  7. Lean protein foods: Lean protein foods are an important part of a healthy diet.
    • lentils
    • chickpeas
    • kidney beans
    • black beans
    • pinto beans
    • almonds
    • walnuts
    • pistachios
    • hazelnuts
    • cashews

Food to Avoid or to Lessen

  1. Red meat, especially processed meats like bacon and sausages
    • veal
    • beef
    • mutton (lamb)
    • pork
    • goat
    • venison
    • horse
    • deer
  2. Desserts, sweets, and sugary beverages
    • soda
    • fruit juice
    • sports juice
    • energy drinks
  3. Refined grains
    • white rice
    • white flour
    • white pasta
    • corn grits
    • crackers
    • cakes
    • pastries
  4. Processed, fried, and fast foods
    • sausage
    • bacon
    • ham
    • deli meats like salami
    • canned meats like corned beef
    • pates
    • soda
    • chips
    • cookies
    • candy
    • breakfast cereals
    • burgers
    • pizza
    • chicken nuggets
    • french fries
    • white flour bread
    • cake
    • hotdogs
  5. Alcohol
